International Opportunities
UVU students have done senior capstone projects in France, Italy, China, Russia, Namibia, and Israel. There is also an exchange program set up with St. Pollen’s University outside of Vienna, Austria.

Each fall students can travel to California to visit studios and learn more about the industry. Some studios that UVU students have visited include Disney Studios, Warner Brothers Studio, Sony/Paramount Studios, and Technicolor.

Broadcast Services
Previously in conjunction with UVU Radio, broadcasting services handle all of the visual, auditory, and video broadcasting at UVU. Students that want to get involved or utilize Broadcasting Services can speak with Broadcasting Admin or Audio Faculty.
Audio Lab
This lab is where most audio classes are held. It is equipped with industry-standard software and connects to a recording studio.
Students can learn about how to manage a radio station including the infrastructure and frameworks that are necessary. UVU Radio is a project students can help with to gain hands-on experience with this type of audio work.
Audio Club
There is a club for students interested in audio projects and education. Connecting with the club can help students network and find internships or project ideas.

UX Design Lab
This lab is equipped with several large whiteboards covering three walls, whiteboard-top tables, and a variety of smart technology, including a lock, doorbell, thermostat, home hubs, refrigerator, frame, and TV. This lab is designed for UX student projects on smart technology and designing sessions and wireframing. The lab schedule is posted outside the door.
Usability Research Lab
This lab is designed for user testing and usability research. It is equipped with long tables, streaming technology, an eye-tracking equipment station, and swiveling monitors mounted to the tables. The lab schedule is posted outside the door.
The Interaction Design emphasis touches on basic VR and AR principles with projects such as VR tours, print materials with AR elements, and immersive experiences using VR and AR features.

UXpeditions
A few times each year, the program runs trips called UXpeditions, taking students around the country and around the world to learn about Interaction Design, UX, and how technology is being used in the Web Design and Development field. Recent trip destinations include Austin, Amsterdam, and Indianapolis.
International Projects
The Web Design and Development degree program has many opportunities for worldwide travel and international project work. Recent and ongoing international projects include Normandy, Beit Lehi, Alaska, Namibia, and others. There are also often conferences or UX events across the world such as those included in UXpeditions.
Students can attend various conferences throughout the year to learn more about the field and network with working professionals including Adobe Max, Utah Front, and International Student Projects.
