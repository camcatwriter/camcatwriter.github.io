July 19 at 17:21:50 UTC, Apollo 11 passed behind the Moon and entered lunar orbit. They orbited the Moon 30 times before they would start the descent and attempt the landing.
Apollo 11 entered near-circular Earth orbit about 12 minutes into the flight. They orbited Earth one and a half times before the third-stage engines engaged again and the Columbia separated from the used engines, sending the Columbia and Eagle towards the Moon and the used engines into orbit around the Sun.
2 minutes and 42 seconds into the flight, the first-stage engines shutdown and the second-stage engines engaged and ignited. The second-stage engines cut off and separated at about 9 minutes and 8 seconds, allowing the third-stage engines to engage.
