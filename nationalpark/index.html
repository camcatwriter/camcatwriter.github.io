Animals

Leopard Frog

Amphibians may be the last thing people think of when they visit Canyonlands. However, the park is home to a variety of frogs and toads, as well as one species of salamander. Because of this sensitivity, amphibians have become “indicator species,” the health of which can be used to measure the health of an ecosystem. In Canyonlands, amphibian populations are greatest along small perennial streams like those in Horseshoe Canyon and Salt Creek Canyon.

Canyon Wren

Birds are the most visible animals in Canyonlands. Even on the hottest summer day, turkey vultures and white-throated swifts circle above the canyons. During winter, juncos and white-crowned sparrows forage around trees and shrubs. While Canyonlands may not be considered a bird watching hot spot, 273 species have been seen in the park. Canyonlands monitors bird populations at several selected locations in both upland and riparian areas.

Mule Deer

Almost 50 species of mammal are known to live in Canyonlands. Some animals, like desert cottontails, kangaroo rats and mule deer, are common and may be seen by a majority of visitors. Tracks and scat are the most common signs of an animal’s presence. Larger mammals, like mule deer and mountain lions, must cover more territory in order to find food and water, and sometimes migrate to nearby mountains during summer.
